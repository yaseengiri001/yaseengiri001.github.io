Plan around the festivals

Or better, plan into them. Flights fill early around the big ones.

Dashainlate Sep – Oct

Nepal's biggest festival: 15 days of family homecomings, kites, and goat curry. Cities quiet, flights full: book transport early.

TiharOct – Nov

The festival of lights: marigold garlands everywhere, dogs honored with tika on day 2 (yes, really), Deusi-Bhailo singing at night.

HoliFeb – Mar

Colors and water in the streets. Wear clothes you'll surrender. Thamel and Basantapur go fully chromatic.

Indra JatraSep

Kathmandu's chariot festival: the living goddess Kumari processes through Basantapur. Crowded, electric, unmissable.

Buddha JayantiMay full moon

Boudhanath and Swayambhu glow with thousands of butter lamps. The kora at dusk is unforgettable.

LhosarDec – Feb (community-specific)

Tamang, Sherpa and Gurung new years: masked dances at monasteries, especially around Boudha.
