Siddha Gufa caving

Siddha Gufa, below Bandipur

Nepal's largest cave opens in the hillside below Bandipur: a cathedral chamber of stalactites, horseshoe bats and, when the headlamps click off together, the thickest dark you will ever stand in.

A stepped forest path drops for about forty minutes from Bandipur to a modest mouth in the hillside, which opens into the largest cave chamber in the country. Headlamps pick out melted-looking flowstone, a shifting ceiling of horseshoe bats and the small Shiva shrine that earns the cave its name. The guide knows where the easy floor ends and the slick scrambles begin, and calls the lights-out moment at the heart of the chamber, where the dark is total.

  • Half day
  • Bandipur bazaar
  • Oct–Apr
  • any fitness

What this is not

This is not a floodlit show cave: light is whatever you carry, the clay gets on everything, and the bats fly close enough to fan your face.

What's included

  • Headlamps and helmets
  • Local caving guide
  • Cave entry ticket

Good to know. The path down is steep and the cave floor is wet clay, so shoes with grip matter more than anything else you pack. It stays cool inside whatever the season.
