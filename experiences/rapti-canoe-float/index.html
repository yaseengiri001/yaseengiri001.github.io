Rapti canoe float

Rapti River, Sauraha, Chitwan

A dawn drift down the Rapti in a carved dugout, past gharial warming their needle snouts on the sandbars.

You push off from Sauraha ghat while the Rapti is still smoking with mist, in a dugout cut from a single silk-cotton trunk. The boatman poles; nobody talks. Gharial lie on the sandbars with their needle snouts agape to warm up, and mugger crocodiles sit lower in the murk, all eyes. Kingfishers work the banks, ruddy shelducks argue midstream, and sometimes a rhino stands in the current ahead, in which case the boatman waits. The float ends in the buffer zone with a short walk back.

  • 2 hours
  • Sauraha ghat, Chitwan
  • Oct–Mar
  • any fitness

What this is not

This is not a paddle sport: you sit low and still while the boatman poles, and he, not you, sets the distance from every crocodile and rhino.

What's included

  • Buffer-zone entry fee
  • Dugout canoe with an experienced boatman
  • Naturalist guide on board
  • Guided walk back along the bank

Good to know. Winter dawns on the water are cold; bring a layer for the first hour. Gharial are close to reliable in the dry months, a rhino at the water's edge is a bonus, never a promise. Life jackets provided and worn.
