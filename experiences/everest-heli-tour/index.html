Everest Base Camp heli tour

Departs Kathmandu

Helicopter to the Khumbu with a landing at Kala Patthar for the close-up of Everest, and breakfast at Hotel Everest View on the way back.

The fastest way to stand among the Everest giants. A helicopter lifts from Kathmandu, refuels at Lukla, and flies you up the Khumbu valley to a landing near Kala Patthar or Everest Base Camp at well over 5,000 m, the close-up most people walk twelve days for. The air up there is razor-thin, so the landing stays deliberately brief, and then you drop to Hotel Everest View for breakfast with Ama Dablam framed in the window. Shared charters keep the cost down; a private flight gives you the whole cabin and the timing.

  • About 4 hours
  • Kathmandu domestic terminal
  • Oct–Nov · Mar–Apr
  • any fitness

What this is not

This is not a long visit: the high landing lasts minutes to keep the altitude safe, and weather can reroute the whole morning.

What's included

  • Helicopter from Kathmandu, return
  • Landing near Kala Patthar / Base Camp
  • Breakfast at Hotel Everest View
  • Oxygen on board and a flight crew briefing

Good to know. The high landing is short to manage altitude, and weather can reroute or cancel the day. Shared seats are far cheaper than a private charter.
