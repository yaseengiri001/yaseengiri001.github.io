Tatopani hot springs

Tatopani, Kali Gandaki valley

Tatopani means hot water in Nepali, and the village delivers exactly that: steaming riverside pools where trekkers' legs have been recovering for decades.

Tatopani sits low in the Kali Gandaki gorge on the old Annapurna Circuit trail, and its name is a plain description: hot water. The pools are simple concrete tanks by the river, fed by a spring that comes out steaming, and the ritual is unchanged: ease in, gasp, go still. Around you are trekkers comparing knees, local families on an outing, and steam drifting against the gorge wall. The mineral smell of the water stays on your skin for the rest of the day.

  • Half day
  • Tatopani bus stop, Beni road
  • Oct–Apr
  • any fitness

What this is not

This is not a spa in the resort sense: Tatopani is two concrete pools by a working road, and that is its charm.

What's included

  • Pool entry fee
  • Locker and changing shed
  • Milk tea at a gorge-side lodge afterwards

Good to know. The pools are communal and simple: modest swimwear, a basic changing shed, and busy evenings when trekking groups roll in. The spring runs hot, so enter slowly.
