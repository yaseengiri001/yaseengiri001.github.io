New

Nepal's wild far-eastern corner: just over two weeks to the north base camp of the world's third-highest mountain, through rhododendron jungle, Limbu and Tibetan villages, and almost no other trekkers.

Kanchenjunga sits in the remote north-east, hard against Sikkim, and getting there is half the trek: a long drive or flight east, then days through leech-warm jungle and terraced Limbu country before the land tilts up toward the high Ghunsa valley. The north base camp at Pangpema, 5,140 m, faces a two-kilometre wall of ice. This is a restricted area, so a registered guide and a partner are required, and that is exactly why the trail stays empty and the villages stay themselves.

What this is not

This is not a casual undertaking: three weeks, basic food and lodging, long jeep approaches, and help is far away if anything goes wrong.

The route's honest rules3 facts · dated

Restricted area: agency + guide mandatoryas of 2026-03

The permit itself is only issued to a registered agency with a licensed guide. Solo travelers are allowed through an agency since March 2026 (the two-trekker minimum is gone). We file everything.

2 permits, filed by usas of 2026

Restricted permits go through the Department of Immigration: about 1-2 working days with your passport, closed Saturdays and festival windows. We time the filing.

Highlights

Why this one

Safety, plainly

Remote and high: this is a restricted area needing a registered guide and a two-trekker minimum, both included. Rescue is hours or days away, so the itinerary is acclimatisation-first and your guide carries a pulse oximeter and a comms device.

Fitness, honestly

Real hill fitness and stamina for two weeks of back-to-back days, some of them long. No technical climbing, but the remoteness demands self-sufficiency.
