Kanchenjunga Base Camp

कञ्चनजङ्घा आधार शिविर

Example route. This page shows how a verified Levgonepal trek reads. The day-by-day plan, prices and operations here are illustrative, not a live booking.

New
20 days 220 km max 5,140 m 3,500 m ascent strenuous Mar–May · Oct–Nov

Nepal's wild far-eastern corner: three weeks to the north base camp of the world's third-highest mountain, through rhododendron jungle, Limbu and Tibetan villages, and almost no other trekkers.

Kanchenjunga sits in the remote north-east, hard against Sikkim, and getting there is half the trek: a long drive or flight east, then days through leech-warm jungle and terraced Limbu country before the land tilts up toward the high Ghunsa valley. The north base camp at Pangpema, 5,140 m, faces a two-kilometre wall of ice. This is a restricted area, so a registered guide and a partner are required, and that is exactly why the trail stays empty and the villages stay themselves.

Highlights

Why this one

  • Pangpema (5,140 m) under Kanchenjunga's north face
  • Three weeks with almost no other trekkers
  • Limbu and Tibetan villages largely untouched by tourism
  • Rhododendron and old-growth forest of the conservation area
  • Red panda country, if you are very lucky and very quiet

Acclimatisation

The altitude story

Sleep altitudes, day by day. The dips are deliberate: climb high, sleep low is how your blood learns thin air.

Acclimatisation check: the steepest sleep gain is +1100 m (day 15): above 500 m, so the itinerary builds in an acclimatisation day around it.

Safety, plainly

Remote and high: this is a restricted area needing a registered guide and a two-trekker minimum, both included. Rescue is hours or days away, so the itinerary is acclimatisation-first and your guide carries a pulse oximeter and a comms device.

Fitness, honestly

Real hill fitness and stamina for 18 to 20 days back to back, some of them long. No technical climbing, but the remoteness demands self-sufficiency.
