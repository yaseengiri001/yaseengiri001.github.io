Gokyo Lakes

गोक्यो ताल

Example route. This page shows how a verified Levgonepal trek reads. The day-by-day plan, prices and operations here are illustrative, not a live booking.

New
12 days 92 km max 5,357 m 2,497 m ascent demanding Mar–May · Oct–Nov

The Everest region's quieter half: a string of six sacred turquoise lakes under Cho Oyu, and a sunrise from Gokyo Ri that rivals Kala Patthar with a tenth of the crowd.

Most people walk to Base Camp and never know what they missed one valley west. The Gokyo trail leaves the Everest highway at Namche and climbs the Dudh Koshi's far branch, past Dole and Machhermo, to a chain of glacial lakes so blue they look retouched. The climb up Gokyo Ri at dawn gives you four eight-thousanders in one sweep, Everest, Lhotse, Makalu and Cho Oyu, above the largest glacier in Nepal, the Ngozumpa. It is the same altitude discipline as Base Camp, with more lake and less queue.

Highlights

Why this one

  • Sunrise from Gokyo Ri (5,357 m): four 8,000ers at once
  • The six Gokyo lakes, sacred and glacier-fed
  • The Ngozumpa, the longest glacier in Nepal
  • Cho Oyu (8,188 m) filling the head of the valley
  • Far fewer trekkers than the Base Camp trail

Acclimatisation

The altitude story

Sleep altitudes, day by day. The dips are deliberate: climb high, sleep low is how your blood learns thin air.

Acclimatisation check: the steepest sleep gain is +830 m (day 2): above 500 m, so the itinerary builds in an acclimatisation day around it.

Safety, plainly

Same altitude maths as Base Camp: no more than ~500 m of sleeping gain a day above 3,000 m, a full rest day in Namche, and your guide carries a pulse oximeter with twice-daily checks.

Fitness, honestly

Comfortable on 5–7 hour days with a daypack, back to back. No technical skill, just patience with the thin air.
