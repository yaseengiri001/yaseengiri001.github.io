Everest Base Camp

सगरमाथा आधार शिविर

Example route. This page shows how a verified Levgonepal trek reads. The day-by-day plan, prices and operations here are illustrative, not a live booking.

4.7(3)
12 days 130 km max 5,545 m 4,248 m ascent demanding Mar–May · Oct–Nov

Twelve days through Sherpa country to the foot of the world's highest mountain, and the sunrise of your life from Kala Patthar.

The Khumbu doesn't ease you in: the flight to Lukla lands on a runway tilted into a hillside, and from there the trail climbs through pine forest, across swaying suspension bridges, into a world where every village has a gompa and every ridge a view of Ama Dablam. You walk slowly (that's the whole skill) sleeping twice in Namche and twice in Dingboche so your blood learns the altitude. The reward is standing in the amphitheatre of the Khumbu Icefall, and the next dawn watching Everest turn marigold from Kala Patthar.

Acclimatisation

The altitude story

Sleep altitudes, day by day. The dips are deliberate: climb high, sleep low is how your blood learns thin air.

Acclimatisation check: the steepest sleep gain is +830 m (day 2): above 500 m, so the itinerary builds in an acclimatisation day around it.

Itinerary

Day by day

1

Fly to Lukla, walk to Phakding

8 km · 3–4 h · sleep 2,610 m

The famous 30-minute flight, then a gentle warm-up descent along the Dudh Koshi. Don't rush, your body starts adapting today.

2

Phakding → Namche Bazaar

11 km · 6 h · sleep 3,440 m

Five suspension bridges and the long Namche hill. First glimpse of Everest if the sky is kind.

3

Acclimatisation in Namche

5 km · 3 h · sleep 3,440 m

Hike high, sleep low: Everest View Hotel (3,880 m) for tea, back down for the night. Visit the Sherpa Culture Museum.

4

Namche → Tengboche

10 km · 5 h · sleep 3,860 m

Contour trail with Ama Dablam ahead all afternoon. Evening prayers at the monastery are open to guests.

5

Tengboche → Dingboche

11 km · 5–6 h · sleep 4,410 m

Above the treeline now. The valley opens wide and the air gets noticeably thinner.

6

Acclimatisation in Dingboche

5 km · 4 h · sleep 4,410 m

Climb Nangkartshang ridge (5,083 m) slowly, then rest. Headache that won't quit? Tell your guide. Today, not tomorrow.

7

Dingboche → Lobuche

8.5 km · 5 h · sleep 4,940 m

Past the climbers' memorials at Thukla Pass, a quiet, important place. Short steps, steady breathing.

8

Lobuche → Base Camp via Gorak Shep

13 km · 7–8 h · sleep 5,164 m

Drop bags in Gorak Shep, then the rocky walk to Base Camp itself (5,364 m). The Icefall cracks and groans. You'll hear it.

9

Kala Patthar sunrise → Pheriche

12 km · 7 h · sleep 4,371 m

Pre-dawn start, –10 °C, completely worth it: the only place Everest's summit fills the sky. Then a long, happy descent.

10

Pheriche → Namche

15 km · 6–7 h · sleep 3,440 m

Thick air feels like a luxury. Celebrate with a bakery stop.

11

Namche → Lukla

19 km · 7 h · sleep 2,860 m

The last bridges, one final hill into Lukla, and a toast with your crew.

12

Fly back to Kathmandu

sleep 1,400 m

Morning flight out (weather permitting, we build in a buffer). Hot shower, big dinner, done.

Safety, plainly

Altitude is the real risk here, not the trail. Our itinerary follows the golden rule, never raise your sleeping altitude by more than ~500 m above 3,000 m, and your guide carries a pulse oximeter and checks you twice daily.

Fitness, honestly

You should be comfortable walking 5–7 hours with a daypack on consecutive days. No climbing skills needed. It's a walk, just a high one.
