New

A steep-sided Sherpa beyul under the twin summits of Gauri Shankar: river gorge, then Beding's stone houses, then the grey moraine of Tsho Rolpa, Nepal's largest glacial lake. The valley has sent generations of climbing Sherpas to Everest.

What this is not

This is not a placid lake walk: Tsho Rolpa is rigged with early-warning sirens because it could burst, and the trail exists partly because the danger does.

Safety, plainly

Altitude arrives fast: the itinerary sleeps at Na before the lake, and turning back is the treatment if symptoms build. Sections of the lower gorge cross old landslide scars, best walked early in the day.

Fitness, honestly

Big height gains on rough stone staircases from the first afternoon; five to seven hour days that reward genuine hill fitness.
