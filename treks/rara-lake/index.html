4.5(2)

Nepal's biggest lake, hidden in its far-west pine forests: two flights, a short walk, and a blue you won't believe.

Rara is the trip for people who think they've seen Nepal. Two small flights hop you to Talcha airstrip in the Karnali (the country's emptiest, highest-poverty, most beautiful province) and a half-day walk delivers you to a three-by-five-kilometre lake that changes colour hourly: slate at dawn, turquoise at noon, ink by dusk. Horses graze the meadows. Phones stop working. That's the feature, not the bug.

What this is not

This is not an easy add-on: reaching Mugu costs real time or a small plane, and services around the lake stay basic.

The route's honest rules3 facts · dated

Restricted area: agency + guide mandatoryas of 2026-03

The permit itself is only issued to a registered agency with a licensed guide. Solo travelers are allowed through an agency since March 2026 (the two-trekker minimum is gone). We file everything.

1 permit, filed by usas of 2026

Restricted permits go through the Department of Immigration: about 1-2 working days with your passport, closed Saturdays and festival windows. We time the filing.

Acclimatization curve: safe

Sleeping altitudes climb inside the 500 m-a-day guardrail with real rest built in.

Highlights

Why this one

  • The 'Queen of Lakes': 167 m deep, impossibly blue
  • Murma Top sunrise over the whole lake basin
  • Snow-pine forests full of musk deer and red panda (if you're lucky)
  • Boating with the national park's wooden ferry
  • True far-west Nepal: almost zero tourists
