Mardi Himal

मार्दी हिमाल

Example route. This page shows how a verified Levgonepal trek reads. The day-by-day plan, prices and operations here are illustrative, not a live booking.

5.0(2)
4 days 35 km max 4,500 m 1,980 m ascent moderate Mar–May · Oct–Dec

Four days on a rhododendron ridge straight at the Fishtail: the best short trek in Nepal, and still strangely quiet.

Mardi is what trekkers recommend to friends in a low voice, hoping it stays uncrowded. From Pokhara you're in forest within an hour (moss, ferns, red rhododendron in spring) and the whole route simply follows one rising ridge. Each morning the wall of Machhapuchhre is closer, until at Base Camp it fills half the sky. Down in two days via stone villages where the daal bhaat is cooked on wood fires.

Highlights

Why this one

  • Machhapuchhre (Fishtail) close enough to touch
  • Rhododendron tunnels in March–April
  • Sunrise at 4,500 m without a single permit queue
  • Siding village descent: old Nepal, no road noise
  • Doable in a long weekend from Pokhara

Acclimatisation

The altitude story

Sleep altitudes, day by day. The dips are deliberate: climb high, sleep low is how your blood learns thin air.

Acclimatisation check: the steepest sleep gain is +1060 m (day 2): above 500 m, so the itinerary builds in an acclimatisation day around it.

Itinerary

Day by day

1

Pokhara → Kande → Forest Camp

9 km · 5 h · sleep 2,520 m

Short drive, then up through Australian Camp into deep forest. Listen for langur monkeys around dusk.

2

Forest Camp → High Camp

8 km · 5 h · sleep 3,580 m

You break the treeline mid-afternoon and the ridge reveals itself. Sunset here is the trip's first jaw-drop.

3

Base Camp sunrise → Low Camp

11 km · 7 h · sleep 2,970 m

3:30 a.m. headlamps to Base Camp (4,500 m) for sunrise on Mardi, Machhapuchhre and Annapurna South. Long descent after breakfast.

4

Low Camp → Siding → Pokhara

7 km · 4 h · sleep 822 m

Stone steps into Siding village, jeep along the river, Lakeside by mid-afternoon.

Safety, plainly

The summit morning gains a lot of height quickly. If you feel altitude symptoms at High Camp, the view from there is already worth the trip. Don't push past a headache.

Fitness, honestly

Regular hill-walking fitness is plenty. Day 3 is long; the rest are half-days.
