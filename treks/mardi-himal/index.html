5.0(2)

Four days on a rhododendron ridge straight at the Fishtail: the best short trek in Nepal, and still strangely quiet.

Mardi is what trekkers recommend to friends in a low voice, hoping it stays uncrowded. From Pokhara you're in forest within an hour (moss, ferns, red rhododendron in spring) and the whole route simply follows one rising ridge. Each morning the wall of Machhapuchhre is closer, until at Base Camp it fills half the sky. Down in two days via stone villages where the daal bhaat is cooked on wood fires.

What this is not

This is not a gentle beginner stroll: the ridge days are steep and weather-exposed, and High Camp nights are cold even in spring.

The route's honest rules3 facts · dated

Guide rule: required on paper, uneven at checkpointsas of 2025-12

The national rule requires a licensed guide through a registered agency; enforcement varies post to post. Your plan includes one either way, so nothing hangs on a checkpoint's mood.

2 permits, filed by usas of 2026

Standard permits issue same-day in Kathmandu or Pokhara. You carry nothing but the trek.

Day 2 climbs fast: 2,520 m to 3,580 m

Above 3,000 m we hold sleeping-altitude gains near 500 m a day. Ask the planner for the variant with an extra night; your locked blocks stay put.

Safety, plainly

The summit morning gains a lot of height quickly. If you feel altitude symptoms at High Camp, the view from there is already worth the trip. Don't push past a headache.

Fitness, honestly

Regular hill-walking fitness is plenty. Day 3 is long; the rest are half-days.
