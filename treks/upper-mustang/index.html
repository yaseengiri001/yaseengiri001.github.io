New

The forbidden kingdom of Lo: a walled medieval capital, sky-caves and ochre canyons in the rain shadow behind Annapurna and Dhaulagiri, where Tibetan Buddhism lives on untouched.

Closed to outsiders until 1992 and still capped by a costly permit, Upper Mustang is the old Kingdom of Lo, a slice of the Tibetan plateau that happens to sit inside Nepal. The trail north from Kagbeni leaves the green behind and enters a desert of wind-carved cliffs, eroded mani walls and whitewashed villages, climbing to the still-walled capital of Lo Manthang and its 15th-century monasteries. Because it lives in the Himalaya's rain shadow, it is one of the few great treks you can do through the monsoon.

What this is not

This is not a budget trek: the restricted permit alone costs USD 500 for ten days, and the walled kingdom is wind, dust and rock, not forest and rhododendron.

The route's honest rules4 facts · dated

Restricted area: agency + guide mandatoryas of 2026-03

The permit itself is only issued to a registered agency with a licensed guide. Solo travelers are allowed through an agency since March 2026 (the two-trekker minimum is gone). We file everything.

2 permits, filed by usas of 2026

Restricted permits go through the Department of Immigration: about 1-2 working days with your passport, closed Saturdays and festival windows. We time the filing.

Upper Mustang fee changedas of 2025-11

The old USD 500 ten-day permit was scrapped in November 2025; it is now a flat USD 50 per day with no minimum. Older blogs overquote it.

Acclimatization curve: safe

Sleeping altitudes climb inside the 500 m-a-day guardrail with real rest built in.

Safety, plainly

Altitude is gentle here, but the restricted permit requires a registered guide and a minimum of two trekkers, both included. Afternoons bring fierce valley wind; we walk mornings.

Fitness, honestly

Moderate days at modest altitude on dusty jeep-track and trail. The challenge is distance and wind, not steepness.
